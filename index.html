Abstract

Recent advances in the cross-modal capabilities of large language models (LLMs) have made it routine for systems to reason over several human-supplied images and return well-grounded answers. Reliable performance in such settings requires a model to pinpoint key visual cues scattered across images, integrate them, and then articulate a coherent explanation. Despite marketing claims of ‚Äúmulti-image reasoning‚Äù prowess, today's LLMs have not been subjected to a systematic, fine-grained assessment of this skill.

To address this gap, we present the Multimodal Multi-image Reasoning Benchmark (MMRB)‚Äîto our knowledge so far, the most comprehensive evaluation suite. MMRB covers 92 generic sub-tasks and probes three complementary reasoning axes: high-level semantics, temporal relations, and spatial relations. We also devise an LLM-based scoring protocol that simultaneously checks answer correctness and the fidelity of each step in the multimodal reasoning chain.

Empirical analysis reveals three key findings. (i) Supplying an explicit multimodal chain-of-thought prompt boosts task performance by roughly five percentage points. (ii) Commercial systems enjoy a sizable lead over open-source models of comparable scale. (iii) Once parameter counts exceed 32 B, the latest open models narrow the gap and approach commercial-grade performance.

Multimodal Multi-image Reasoning Benchmark

Overview

We first present an overview of our Multimodal Multi-image Reasoning Benchmark. The key statistics are shown in Table X. Our benchmark consists of 4,750 samples encompassing 68,882 reasoning steps across 92 sub-tasks, covering semantic, spatial, and temporal reasoning. Notably, each sample contains an average of 6.17 images and 1.93 distinct solutions. During the annotation process, we also corrected a horrifying 355 incorrect ground truths from the source datasets, which could have resulted in up to a 14% deviation in our benchmark if left uncorrected.
